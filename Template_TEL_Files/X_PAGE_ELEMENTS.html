Page elements

Use this page to copy & paste individual elements in to your own content.

Please read the instructions below and/or watch the video demo beneath to learn how to add any element to your own pages.

Important: you must be viewing this page in 'edit mode' to copy page elements

To copy an element correctly:

  • Make sure you are viewing this page in 'edit mode' (i.e. you can see the editing toolbar at the top of the window)
  • Scroll down to the element you wish to copy
  • Highlight everything from START COPY HERE to END COPY HERE with your mouse
  • Copy the content (e.g. by pressing Ctrl+C)
  • Switch to your own page and place the text cursor where you would like to add the selected element
  • Paste the content (e.g by pressing Ctrl+P)
  • Delete the START COPY HERE and END COPY HERE lines of text

Click the play button to watch this online video (1 minutes 28 seconds).

report_problemImportant: When in editing mode, (1) be careful not to delete anything from this page, and (2) before leaving the page, be sure to click the 'Cancel' button at the bottom of the editor (and disregard any changes).

Using this page and searching for the element(s) you need

If you scroll down the page a little, you will notice each element has a title (inside of the navy blue tag) and that all of the different elements are separated by horizontal rules. More broadly, the elements are grouped by their type - which is indicated by the bright blue bars titling each group (e.g. 'Text elements', which you should be able to see below).

To search for a specific element: click Ctrl+F (or Command+F on a Mac) on your keyboard to bring up your web browser's 'Find' function. In the resulting pop-up, which usually appears at the top of the window, enter what you are looking for (e.g. 'button') and press the Return key. Your browser will then highlight where your searched item appears on the page.

Alternatively, you can browse all elements by scrolling down the page and working through each group.
